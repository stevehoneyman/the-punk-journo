Henry McDonald is a critcally acclaimed author & journalist with a career spanning over twenty five years

Henry McDonald has been there, seen it and done it.

Growing up amidst the troubles of Northern Ireland, serving twenty years as a staff writer at the Guardian Observer and the author of several books, Henry McDonald is the punk journo.

'Two souls, alas, are housed within my breast, and each will wrestle for the mastery there.'

Robbie McManus is tortured. His psychopathic comrade 'Padre Pio' McCann is never far from wreaking havoc, his punk cousin 'Rex Mundi' has arrived from England and is getting in the way, his father is imploring him to finish his A-levels and get the hell out of Belfast - and then there's Sabine, the mysterious loner in The Pound who shimmers, trancelike, on the dancefloor to the opening track of David Bowie's Low.
